Dear Niket,

Sometimes I think back to a few months ago and forget how much has changed in such a short time. As I laid in bikram class this evening, it seemed like just yesterday you were right there next to me, grunting about the pose where we have to tilt our entire upper body sidewards, or hushing people in the class for talking during the session. You and I were a badass duo of 'doers', set on making our mark on the world.

Then, cancer struck reality in a cyclone-like tear and threw everything momentarily into disarray. I know this was extra tough on an energetic guy like yourself because you're so used to telling reality 'fuck you' and bucking the trend. In many ways, we tend to live our lives with this 'so what if i die' mentality (you electing to ride a motorcycle, etc.) but this was truly a confrontation with that belief. You've had to fight a lot of things that no one would ever imagine a 25-year old going through, and I'm sure it's given you a lot of time (more than you'd like) to reflect on where you are and where you want to head. I look forward to having you back in our lives; you were already wise before but you now have perspective on things that only ones who are facing finality might come to grips with. I know this will only drive you to push more impact on the world in your endeavors, whether it be an innovation or simply a smile on someone's face. You've already proven to be quite good at both.

Now, we're at the last chemotherapy session and your life will slowly begin to return to its normal state of being. Although it's awful that you had to be put through this in the first place, I hope you realized how many people were on your side of the ring as the war raged on during each surgery, CT, IV, injection. It was this lone, malignant anomaly facing a mountain of support, love and outpoor of friends and family. This sort of thing isn't a luxury every cancer patient is graced with, but rather a testament to you as a person and the lasting impact you have on others. I've read through lettersfromniket.com more than I can count now, and always leave moved. (thanks Nat!)

I'm so proud of you for enduring this journey that no one would ever wish upon another. You are (and were) an inspiration in my life and I can't wait to have you back man. I love you like a brother, and am so happy to hear you gave cancer a big 'fuck you'. But, like most of us thought from the start... Come on, it's Niket, of course he's going to make it.

Here's to you, Niket.

Michael Mizono
